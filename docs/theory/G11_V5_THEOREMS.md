## 1. Common finite-grid setup

At hierarchy level `ell`, let the standardized target input be

`X_ell ~ P_ell = N(0,I_dell)`.

The deterministic defensive proposal is

`Q_ell = sum_j pi_(ell,j) N(m_(ell,j),I_dell)`,

where `pi_(ell,j)>0`, the weights sum to one, and one component has zero mean with
total weight `delta_ell>0`. The price-driver portions of all means lie in one common
oriented positive rank-one span `span(U_ell)`.

Write

`X_ell = U_ell Z_ell + R_ell`,

with `U_ell` orthonormal, `Z_ell` standard normal under the target, and `R_ell`
orthogonal to the span. Proposal controls are deterministic; feedback controls are
outside this theorem.

All main V5 estimands are probabilities on a declared finest finite grid `L`.

## 2. Theorem V5-1: exact full and residual balance likelihoods

The full target-over-proposal likelihood is

`L_ell(x) = [sum_j pi_j exp(m_j^T x-||m_j||^2/2)]^-1`.

Decompose `m_j=U b_j+c_j` with `c_j` orthogonal to `U`. After integrating the span
coordinate, the residual likelihood is

`Lbar_ell(r) = [sum_j pi_j exp(c_j^T r-||c_j||^2/2)]^-1`.

Both satisfy the defensive bounds

`0 < L_ell <= 1/delta_ell`, `0 < Lbar_ell <= 1/delta_ell`.

Status: proved in G11 and independently oracle-tested.

## 3. Theorem V5-2: exact scalar-threshold marginalization

Suppose, conditional on `R`, the finite-grid event is

`F=1{Z<=A(R)}`.

Then

`E_Q[L(X)F | R] = Lbar(R) Phi(A(R))`.

For adjacent fine/coarse events represented by one common coordinate,

`E_Q[L(X)(F_f-F_c) | R]`
`=Lbar(R)(Phi(A_f(R))-Phi(A_c(R)))`.

The proposal conditional law of `Z|R` is not assumed standard normal; the identity
follows by cancellation against the target-over-proposal likelihood.

Status: proved and pathwise tested for terminal, discrete barrier, and the implemented
finite-grid excursion task.

## 4. Theorem V5-3: finite-grid start-level telescoping

For every admissible start `s in {0,...,L}`, define

`Y^(s)=Y_s + sum_(ell=s+1)^L (Y_ell-Y_(ell-1))`,

where every correction is evaluated on its declared common fine probability space
and each term is changed back to its target law by the exact likelihood. Then

`E[Y^(s)] = E_P[F_L]`.

Different deterministic proposals may be used for different start candidates, but
each candidate must retain its own exact likelihood and finest-grid target.

Status: proved for the finite grid and tested.

## 5. Theorem V5-4: pilot-selected estimator remains unbiased

Let `G` be the sigma-field generated by proposal training, selection pilots, and
allocation pilots. Let `S` and the final integer allocations be `G`-measurable.
Assume:

1. every candidate `s` satisfies Theorem V5-3;
2. final samples are independent of `G`; and
3. pilot samples are not inserted into the final means.

Then

`E[hat p_S | G] = p_L`, and therefore `E[hat p_S]=p_L`.

The result also covers a random CEM proposal: conditional on its frozen training
output, exact likelihood evaluation makes the final ordinary IS mean unbiased.

Proof: condition on `G`. The selected candidate and sample counts are fixed, every
final term is an unbiased mean of its target level term, and Theorem V5-3 telescopes.
Apply the tower property. QED.

Status: proved. The V5 runtime now freezes a preparation hash and integer allocation
before allocating any final seed, and bitwise resume tests enforce the separation.

## 6. Theorem V5-5: simultaneous bounded variance intervals

Let `X_1,...,X_n` be IID with `X in [-M,M]`. For a per-interval error probability
`alpha`, Hoeffding gives simultaneous two-sided intervals

`mu in [mean(X)-r_mu, mean(X)+r_mu]`,

where `r_mu=M sqrt(2 log(2/alpha)/n)`, and

`m2 in [mean(X^2)-r_2, mean(X^2)+r_2]`,

where `r_2=M^2 sqrt(log(2/alpha)/(2n))`.

Clamp the second-moment interval to `[0,M^2]`. If the intervals are
`[L_mu,U_mu]` and `[L_2,U_2]`, then on their joint coverage event,

`Var(X) >= max(0,L_2-max(|L_mu|,|U_mu|)^2)`

and

`Var(X) <= max(0,min(M^2,U_2-min_(u in [L_mu,U_mu])u^2))`.

For a finite collection of terms and pilot looks, allocate the familywise error by a
union bound. A fixed-sample interval reused at uncorrected optional looks is invalid.

Status: proved from Hoeffding plus interval arithmetic. The bounds may be
conservative.

## 7. Theorem V5-6: candidate work intervals and regret

For positive variances and deterministic positive costs, the continuous allocation
coefficient

`K_s=(sqrt(V_s C_s)+sum_(ell>s)sqrt(V_Delta,ell C_Delta,ell))^2`

is coordinatewise nondecreasing in every variance and cost. Therefore simultaneous
variance intervals induce valid lower and upper intervals for every candidate total
work after adding known preprocessing work.

If plug-in total works obey

`(1-gamma)W_s <= What_s <= (1+gamma)W_s`

simultaneously for every candidate and `gamma<1`, the plug-in minimizer satisfies

`W_selected/min_s W_s <= (1+gamma)/(1-gamma)`.

Proof: let `s*` minimize true work. Then

`W_selected <= What_selected/(1-gamma)`
`<= What_s*/(1-gamma)`
`<= (1+gamma)W_s*/(1-gamma)`. QED.

Status: proved as a finite-candidate conditional statement. Statistical coverage and
practical regret require qualification.

## 8. Theorem V5-7: margin-localized threshold moment bound

On a good event where fine and coarse active denominators are at least `kappa`, the
candidate ratio error is bounded by

`D_n/kappa + M_c D_b/kappa^2`.

After adding the explicit mesh/rank-enrichment defect `D_mesh`, write the threshold
bound as `B_kappa`. Then

`E_Q[H_DCS^2]`
`<= delta^-1(phi_max^2 E[B_kappa^2 1_G]+P(G^c))`,

while the raw correction has the corresponding linear good-event term.

Status: deterministic and measure-change parts proved in V4. Applying a rate to
rBergomi requires Theorems V5-8 or V5-9.

## 9. Conditional theorem V5-8: terminal rBergomi rate

For the implemented positive control direction, suppose uniformly over levels:

1. direction weights satisfy the required grid-scaled regularity;
2. fine/coarse terminal intercept and slope errors have stated `L^p` rates;
3. numerator envelopes have sufficient moments; and
4. terminal slopes have sufficient negative moments or lower-tail bounds.

Then optimize `kappa(h)` in Theorem V5-7 to obtain the terminal DCS correction upper
rate. A bias/complexity theorem additionally requires a separate weak-bias result.

Status: conditional. Strict finite-grid positivity of the slope is not a uniform
negative-moment proof.

## 10. Conditional theorem V5-9: discrete-barrier rate

In addition to V5-8-type coefficient and slope control on `[tau,T]`, suppose:

1. the maximizing threshold occurs before `tau` only on a controlled bad event; and
2. the new-monitoring-point enrichment defect has the required moment rate.

Then Theorem V5-7 yields a barrier correction upper rate after balancing coefficient,
active-time, small-slope, and enrichment terms.

Status: conditional. A maximum over the coarse candidates alone does not control new
fine monitoring points.

## 11. Continuous-target boundary

None of V5-1--V5-7 proves continuous-time exactness. Terminal continuous-time bias
requires convergence and anti-concentration for the threshold. Barrier continuous
monitoring additionally requires a monitoring-bias theorem. Without those results,
all primary claims remain finite-grid.

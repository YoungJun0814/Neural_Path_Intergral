## 1. Audit decision

**Pass for M0--M1 infrastructure and the finite-grid selector/allocation theorem
chain; conditional for model-level rates; blocked for any continuous-time claim.**

The finite-grid density, marginalization, telescoping, pilot-selection unbiasedness,
bounded variance-interval, and deterministic regret statements are internally
consistent. No terminal or barrier rough-Bergomi rate has been proved by adding those
finite-dimensional results together.

## 2. Independent checks

### 2.1 Likelihood orientation

For `Q_j=N(m_j,I)`, `dQ_j/dP=exp(m_j^T x-||m_j||^2/2)`. The estimator uses the
reciprocal mixture ratio `dP/dQ`. Decision: pass.

### 2.2 Residual cancellation

The proposal conditional distribution of the integrated coordinate is generally a
residual-dependent mixture. The DCS identity is valid because the full likelihood
cancels the proposal joint density before the target Gaussian coordinate is
integrated. Decision: pass.

### 2.3 Defensive bound

A zero-mean component of total weight `delta` gives `q>=delta p` before and after
span marginalization. Decision: pass.

### 2.4 Selected-estimator unbiasedness

Randomly selecting a candidate after pilot inspection is valid only because all
candidates estimate the same finest-grid target and final samples are independent.
Reusing pilot samples in final means would invalidate the short proof. Decision:
pass as a theorem; runtime seed enforcement required.

### 2.5 Hoeffding variance interval

The mean range has width `2M`; the squared-value range has width `M^2`. The stated
radii and variance interval arithmetic are valid after familywise error allocation.
The construction is conservative but not anti-conservative. Decision: pass.

### 2.6 Work monotonicity and regret

The work coefficient is nondecreasing in positive variances and costs. The plug-in
regret proof is a direct three-inequality argument. It is not an unconditional
statistical theorem until simultaneous coverage is supplied. Decision: pass with
claim boundary.

## 3. Central unresolved proof obligations

### 3.1 Direction regularity

The implementation enforces a strictly positive rank-one price direction on every
finite grid. A level-uniform theorem additionally needs grid-scaled upper/lower
regularity of those direction weights. Finite-grid positivity alone is insufficient.

### 3.2 Terminal inverse slope

The terminal slope is a positive weighted sum of square-root variance values. It has
no deterministic positive lower bound. A proof may use lognormal integral inverse
moments, but it must match the BLP discretization and direction normalization.

### 3.3 Coefficient rate

A strong rate for the rough driver does not automatically give the required rate for
the affine threshold numerator and denominator. The exact implemented fine/coarse
coupling must be analyzed.

### 3.4 Barrier active time and enrichment

Very early slopes are small. For a downside barrier below initial spot, early
candidates should normally be inactive, but this requires a probability bound. Fine
grids also add candidate times; common-grid Lipschitz continuity alone misses this
defect.

### 3.5 Continuous monitoring

Finite-grid hard-event exactness says nothing about continuous barrier monitoring.
This claim remains blocked.

## 4. Runtime obligations generated by the proof

1. reject feedback/path-dependent controls in exact DCS mode;
2. validate the one-signed rank-one price span and normalized direction;
3. expose fine/coarse intercepts, slopes, active indices, and enrichment defects;
4. keep every pilot/training/final/reference seed role disjoint;
5. exclude all pilots from final means;
6. divide interval alpha across every term, moment, method, and optional look;
7. use bounded intervals only for actually defensive bounded contributions;
8. retain zero-event upper uncertainty rather than a zero variance;
9. freeze selection and allocation before final seed allocation;
10. serialize censoring and every eliminated candidate; and
11. independently reconstruct final summaries and gates.

## 6. Implementation audit on 2026-07-22

### 6.1 Threshold instrumentation

`rbergomi_threshold_diagnostics.py` reconstructs the estimator's terminal and
barrier thresholds from the exact stored affine intercept/slope arrays. For barriers
it separately records the common-grid candidate error and the nonnegative
fine-only monitoring defect. Initially hit paths retain `+inf` thresholds, receive
active time zero, and are excluded from finite-threshold rate fits rather than being
silently coerced to finite values.

Development smoke evidence and a subsequent clean non-smoke run covered
`H in {0.05,0.12,0.30}` with `eta` and `rho` held fixed. Every pathwise
reconstruction, likelihood, and localized-bound gate passed. The non-smoke DCS
second-moment exponents were approximately `0.044/0.217/0.591` for the terminal
task and `0.047/0.225/0.694` for the barrier task as H increased. These observations
are falsification evidence only; they do not discharge Sections 3.1--3.4.

### 6.2 Sequential crossover

The implementation divides familywise alpha over profiles, two moments, and every
predeclared cumulative look. It rejects an unregistered optional look. A candidate
is eliminated only when its lower total-work bound exceeds the tolerated best upper
bound. Zero observed corrections retain a positive variance upper bound.

The initial 30-repetition-per-case smoke oracle (240 runs) showed simultaneous
coverage `1.0`, no optimal-candidate elimination on a coverage event, median regret
`1.0`, and 90th-percentile regret about `1.2012`. It was subsequently supplemented
by the frozen non-smoke G4 run: 4,000 records from clean commit `a49c51a`, coverage
`0.99875`, zero invalid eliminations on the coverage event, median regret `1.0`, and
90th-percentile regret about `1.2012`. All predeclared G4 gates passed.

### 6.3 Achieved-RMSE execution

Selection and integer allocations are frozen before any final seed is allocated.
The allocation uses simultaneous upper variances, rounds upward, rechecks design
variance, and resource-censors before final sampling if the operation-work cap is
infeasible. Final observations cannot be selection observations. Checkpoints bind
the preparation hash, counts, moments, work ledger, and seed ledger.

The end-to-end rBergomi smoke completed terminal and barrier cells without
censoring, and an independently implemented audit reconstructed their selection,
allocation, work, estimate, variance, and gates. This establishes implementation
consistency, not the 20-cluster primary performance claim.
